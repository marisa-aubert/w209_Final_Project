Coral Bleaching

Visualizing spatiotemporal patterns in coral bleaching and associated environmental stressors

Coral reefs are among the most vibrant and vital ecosystems on our planet — often called the “rainforests of the sea” for their extraordinary biodiversity. Yet today, these ecosystems are under severe threat. Rising ocean temperatures, driven by climate change, are causing widespread coral bleaching, a phenomenon in which corals expel the symbiotic algae that give them color and life. What remains are ghostly white skeletons, signaling stress and the potential for coral death. Studies now estimate that over 50% of the world’s corals have died since 1950 and over 80% have experienced heat stress.

This project explores coral bleaching through interactive data visualizations built from the BCO-DMO coral bleaching environmental dataset, a comprehensive dataset with more than 40,000 observations on corals and surrounding environmental conditions between 1980 and 2020.
